# Elevator Pitch

ARIA-OS is a text-to-machinable-part CAD pipeline. You describe a mechanical part in plain English -- "ratchet ring, 213mm OD, 24 teeth, 21mm thick" -- and ARIA-OS generates a validated STEP file, runs physics checks, uploads it to Onshape for collaborative editing, visually verifies the geometry with a vision LLM, and optionally produces CNC toolpaths, DFM analysis, and instant manufacturing quotes. It replaces the slowest step in hardware development: going from an idea in your head to a real engineering file that a machine shop can cut. The pipeline ships with 39 parametric CadQuery templates covering common mechanical parts (gears, brackets, flanges, housings, nozzles, bearings, heat sinks, and more), a structured spec extractor that parses dimensions from natural language, a multi-retry validation loop with failure-context injection, and deterministic CEM physics checks that enforce safety factors before any file leaves the system. ARIA-OS is built for product designers, machine shops, and hardware startups who need to move from concept to CNC-ready geometry in seconds, not days.
